Amarillo Amber Ale

Ingredients

  • Pilsner malt (4.2kg)
  • aromatic malt (113g)
  • CaraAmber (30 °L) (113g)
  • CaraRed (113g)
  • Cara Munich II (113g)
  • Warrior (14g at 16% alpha acids, 30 min.)
  • Amarillo (28g at 9.5% alpha acids, 10 min.)
  • Amarillo (28g at 9.5% alpha acids, 5 min.)
  • White Labs WLP023 (Burton Ale)

Steps

Mash grains at 67 °C for 60 minutes.Sparge to collect roughly 25 L of wort.Boil for 90 minutes adding hops at times indicated.Cool wort to pitching temperature as quickly as possible, 18–24 °C.Transfer wort to sanitized fermenter then pitch the yeast.Ferment at 18–20 °C.Dry hops are added loose to the carboy and soaked for two weeks after primary fermentation is complete.Bottle as usual.

Guinness Draught clone

Ingredients

  • English 2-row pale ale malt (5.0 lbs.)
  • flaked barley (2.5 lbs.)
  • roasted barley (500 °L) (1.0 lb.)
  • East Kent Goldings (68 g at 5% alpha acids, 60 min.)
  • Wyeast 1084 (Irish Ale)

Steps

Heat 10 L of water to 72 °C and stir in crushed grains and flaked barley.Mash at 66 °C for 60 minutesStir boiling water into grain bed until temperature reaches 76 °C and rest for 5 minutes.Recirculate until wort is clear, then begin running wort off to kettle.Sparge with 77 °C water.Boil wort for 90 minutes, adding hops with 60 minutes left in boil.Cool wort and transfer to fermenter.Aerate wort and pitch yeast.Ferment at 22 °C.Rack to secondary when fermentation is complete.Bottle a few days later, when beer falls clear. If beer is kegged, consider pushing with a nitrogen blend (see the Jan-Feb 2005 issue for more information on this).
